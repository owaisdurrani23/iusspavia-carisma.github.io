CARISMA, within the Educating Future Citizens (EFC) project, is organising a two-day transdisciplinary workshop on integrated flood risk assessment for climate adaptation. The workshop is aimed at PhD students and brings together engineering and economic perspectives to address the growing challenges of climate-driven hydrological risk, a theme central to CARISMA's research mission.

The programme includes high-level keynote lectures from leading international scholars, advanced methodological sessions on infrastructure modelling, systemic risk, behavioural dynamics, and agent-based approaches to flood adaptation, and interactive discussions on research frontiers and policy implications at the interface of physical risk modelling and economic evaluation.

**Invited speakers**

Tatiana Filatova — Professor, Faculty of Technology, Policy and Management, TU Delft

Raghav Pant — Senior Research Associate, Environmental Change Institute, University of Oxford

**When:** 15–16 April 2026

**Where:** Palazzo del Broletto, Piazza della Vittoria 15, Pavia
